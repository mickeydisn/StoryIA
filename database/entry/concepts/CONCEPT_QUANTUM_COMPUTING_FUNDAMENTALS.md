## Basic Information
- **Concept Name**: Quantum Computing Fundamentals
- **Category**: Computer Science / Physics
- **Alternative Names**: Quantum Information Processing, Qubit Computing

## Definition & Description
- **Core Definition**: A computing paradigm that uses quantum mechanical phenomena to perform calculations, enabling massive parallelism through superposition and entanglement.
- **Extended Description**: Unlike classical bits that exist in binary states (0 or 1), quantum bits (qubits) can exist in superposition (both 0 and 1 simultaneously), and can be entangled with other qubits. This allows quantum computers to process vast amounts of data simultaneously and solve certain problems exponentially faster than classical computers.
- **Key Characteristics**:
  - Superposition: Multiple states simultaneously
  - Entanglement: Correlated quantum states
  - Interference: Wave-like behavior of quantum states
  - Measurement collapse: Probabilistic state resolution
- **Scope**: Fundamental quantum information processing
